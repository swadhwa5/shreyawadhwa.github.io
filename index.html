About Me


I am a rising sophomore at Johns Hopkins University, majoring in Computer Science and Cognitive Science. I love sitting and struggling for hours while writing code and I have worked with C, C++, Python, and Java, as well as SQL and R. This summer, I have been learning web development using html/css/javascript and React, and also working on the software development team of Delineo Modelling, a project under Dr. Anton Dahbura, that aims to predict the spread of the Covid-19 in a personalized way for smaller communities.

Since the spring of my freshman year, I have been involved with the Bonner Lab on campus, where I work Dr. Mick Bonner and Dr. Alon Hafri to hypothesize why certain regions of the brain develop 'functional selectivity' to scenes, or to objects. I have had an amazing experience working as a Research Assistant where my work ranges from generating a 3000+ image stimulus set to attending talks and reading papers written by some of the most prominent cognitive scientists in the world. I am passionate about 'Vision' research and I am on my way to find a balance between my love for employing coding to solve real life problems and using the functionality of the human brain to crack the emerging era of Artificial Intelligence!
